Use Case 2

Dynamic Price Prediction

Supervised Learning · Linear Regression

01 · Problem

📋 Problem Description

Modern markets are highly volatile. Setting a static price leads to missed revenue during high demand or lost customers during stiff competition. This system utilizes a Regression Model to calculate the optimal price point by analyzing internal costs and external market pressure, ensuring maximum profit margins without losing market share.

03 · Technical Insight
📐 How the Price Model Works

The model identifies how much each factor "pulls" the price up or down:

Price = β₀ + β₁(Prod. Cost) + β₂(Demand) + β₃(Comp. Index)
β₁ (Cost)
Strongest positive weight. Price must cover production cost.
β₂ (Demand)
Increases price during peak seasons and high market interest.
β₃ (Competition)
Acts as balancer to keep price competitive.
💡 The system automatically adjusts these weights to find the Sweet Spot where maximum revenue aligns with customer willingness to pay.
